Frequently Asked Questions

You Have Questions, We Have Answers

Application for a health insurance plan?

The requirements for applying for a health insurance plan can vary depending on the specific plan. However, the following information is generally required for application:

Basic personal information, such as name, address, date of birth, and social security number

Details about the insured person(s), including age, gender, height, weight, tobacco use, and medical history

Details about the coverage desired, such as deductibles, copayments, and specific benefits or procedures covered

Billing information, including mailing address and method of payment

How can I lower my health insurance premiums?

There are several ways to lower your health insurance premiums. Here are some strategies you can consider:

Quit smoking: Smoking can significantly increase your health insurance premiums. If you're a smoker, quitting can have a significant impact on your costs.

Maintain a healthy weight: Obesity can also increase your health insurance premiums. If you're overweight, working to lose weight can help reduce your premiums.

Enroll in a high-deductible health plan (HDHP): HDHPs often have lower premiums but higher out-of-pocket costs. By choosing this option, you can potentially save money on your health insurance premiums.

What happens if I need medical treatment while abroad?

If you need medical treatment while abroad, it's essential to consider your insurance coverage and the extent of the benefits provided.

We offer international health insurance plans, which cover medical expenses incurred while traveling abroad. These plans may have deductibles and copayments similar to domestic plans, and the coverage provided can vary widely depending on the specific plan.

Depending on your insurance plan and the country you're visiting, you may be responsible for some or all of the out-of-pocket costs associated with your medical treatment. Be sure to review your policy benefits and consult with us before traveling.

How can I check the coverage of my insurance policy?

You can check the coverage of your insurance policy by referring to the policy documents you received through mail or contacting our customer care. If you have a comprehensive insurance policy, it should cover most, if not all, expenses related to a covered incident. However, if you have a limited or exclusive policy, the coverage may be more specific. For example, a limited policy may only cover the insured person, while an exclusive policy may cover only a specific event or set of events.

Can I transfer my insurance policy to another insurance company?

Yes, you can transfer your insurance policy to another insurance company, but this process may involve certain risks and costs. Some insurance companies may charge a premium transfer fee, which can be based on the amount of time the policy has been in force, the amount of premiums paid, or a combination of these factors. Additionally, transferring a policy to another insurance company may require you to undergo a new medical examination, depending on the policy and the new insurance company's requirements.
